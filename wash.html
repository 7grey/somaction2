WASH PROGRAMS

Water, sanitation and hygiene (WASH)

WASH Programs include water infrastructures construction and rehabilitation, sanitation in schools and MCH where latrine construction infrastructure is built and promotion of CLTS triggering the community to eliminate open defecation and educate communities on good hygiene practices promotion. Safe drinking-water, sanitation and hygiene are crucial to human health and well-being. Safe WASH is not only a prerequisite to health, but contributes to livelihoods, school attendance and dignity and helps to create resilient communities living in healthy environments. Drinking unsafe water impairs health through illnesses such as diarrhoea, and untreated excreta contaminates groundwaters and surface waters used for drinking-water, irrigation, bathing and household purposes. Chemical contamination of water continues to pose a health burden, whether natural in origin such as arsenic and fluoride, or anthropogenic such as nitrate. Safe and sufficient WASH plays a key role in preventing numerous NTDs such as trachoma, soil-transmitted helminths and schistosomiasis. Diarrhoeal deaths as a result of inadequate WASH were reduced by half during the Millennium Development Goal (MDG) period (1990–2015), with the significant progress on water and sanitation provision playing a key role. Evidence suggests that improving service levels towards safely managed drinking-water or sanitation such as regulated piped water or connections to sewers with wastewater treatment can dramatically improve health by reducing diarrhoeal disease deaths.

Importance

1. Safe Drinking-Water: Access to clean and safe drinking water is essential. Unsafe water can lead to illnesses like diarrhea. Contaminated water sources can impact both surface and groundwater quality.
2. Sanitation: Proper sanitation facilities, including toilets and waste management systems, are crucial. Untreated excreta can contaminate water sources, affecting health and the environment.
3. Hygiene: Good hygiene practices, such as handwashing, prevent the spread of diseases. Basic hygiene measures are vital in healthcare settings and communities.
4. Impact: Safe WASH contributes to livelihoods, school attendance, and dignity. It also helps create resilient communities living in healthy environments.
