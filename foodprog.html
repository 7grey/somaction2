FOOD DISTRIBUTION PROGRAM

Food Assistance for Assets (FFA) programmes

Food Assistance for Assets (FFA) programmes are one of WFPâ€™s key activities that can contribute to improving the long-term food security of people and their communities.

Through FFA, food insecure households receive cash or food-based transfers to address their immediate food needs, freeing up their time to build or rehabilitate assets, such as constructing a road or rehabilitating degraded land, that will have long-term impacts, creating healthier natural environments, reducing risks and the impact of shocks, increasing food productivity, and strengthening resilience to natural disasters.

The design and planning of FFA activities is highly context based and requires the active participation of communities for its success. In turn, FFA empowers communities to become agents of their own change.

FFA can play a crucial role in the climate change adaptation efforts of communities and, depending on contexts, it often represents the foundation of broader integrated resilience approaches on the ground, that include access to markets and financial services, post-harvest loss management (PHL), school feeding, and anticipatory actions.
