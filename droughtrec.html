Drought Relief and Recovery

Drought relief and recovery programs play a crucial role in supporting communities and regions facing drought crises.

Disaster Assistance Programs: The U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) Farm Service Agency provides financial and technical assistance to drought-affected areas. This includes emergency aid and loans for losses related to crops, livestock, trees, and farmland. You can explore these programs using the Disaster Assistance Discovery Tool.

Environmental Quality Incentives Program (EQIP): EQIP assists agricultural producers by addressing natural resource concerns and delivering environmental benefits. These benefits include improved water and air quality, soil health, wildlife habitat, and drought resilience.

Emergency Watershed Protection Program (EWP): EWP offers technical and financial aid to local communities after natural disasters, including drought. It helps relieve imminent threats to life and property caused by disasters impairing watersheds.

Rural Utilities Service Water and Environmental Programs: Rural communities can obtain technical assistance and financing through this program to develop drinking water and waste disposal systems.

Economic Injury Disaster Loans: The U.S. Small Business Administration provides these loans to businesses, agricultural cooperatives, and nonprofit organizations suffering substantial economic injury in declared disaster areas.
