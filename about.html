BACKGROUND

Soma-Action International Fund

SOMA-ACTION is a Non-Governmental Organization formed in 1992 and based in Elwaq-District Gedo region and Lower Shebelle Region of Somalia. With its main office in Elwak, it has staff in El Waq and Marka Somalia. SOMA-ACTION has over twenty years (20) of experience in designing and implementing community-based relief, rehabilitation, and development and peace projects. One of the major assets of SOMA-ACTION is its qualified professionals who have experience in the fields of program design and implementation. During its existence, the organization has managed to conduct various needs assessments through participatory methods to assess the priority needs of the local people. SOMA-ACTION has a wealth of experience in partnership building and collaborated with various UN agencies, International NGOs, and members of the community to implement programs and achieve its goals.

In 1992 soma-action in collaboration with UNHCR implemented over 48 projects through the Quick impact project for Somali refugees (QUIPS) in ElWak-Somalia and again Returnees to Somalia 1994. In 1999-2007, in collaboration with CARE SOMALIA, SOMA-ACTION implemented a food distribution project (RFSP) Rural food security project in 22 villages with 350MT of food each month in ElWaq Districts. The project was funded by USAID through CARE-SOMALIA for El-Niño affected communities.

In early 1997, SOMA-ACTION field team participated in several organizational capacity-building trainings supported by CARE, UNICEF, UNHCR, WFP, CEWARN (IGAD), UN OCHA, and PACT-KENYA programs in ElWaq. In addition to small grants for office equipment and supplies, the training programs provided soma-action institutional capacity and staff development opportunities. These additional training and facilities gave SOMA-ACTION additional skills to manage its projects and resources even more effectively.

In early 2000 SOMA-ACTION and COSV jointly implemented cash for work program through which communal dams and water catchments in pastoral areas of Lower Shebelle region were rehabilitated. In December 2001 SOMA-ACTION implemented a UNICEF-funded HIV/AIDS awareness program in Elwak towns. A Series of workshops and seminars were organized for various community groups in the Districts.

From March 2008- to July 2009 SOMA-ACTION in collaboration with WFP implemented general food distribution in 24 villages in ElWaq- Somalia district. In early 2009, SOMA-ACTION implemented UNICEF funded Emergency provision of water through water trucking and hygiene promotion and rehabilitation of 18 water sources in El Waq District.

PACT has also given a Training of Trainers course on trauma healing and social reconciliation to Soma Action staff. One trauma workshop was conducted in El Waqntown in collaboration with Gedo Peace Consortium and the response from the participants was overwhelming. They felt that the trauma awareness and healing approach was the best to break the cycle of violence and have people who have been afflicted by violent conflict for 20 years cope and resume a sense of normalcy in their lives. During the workshop, people who were formerly sworn enemies forgave each other, enabling dialogue and social reconciliation. Currently (March 2011), SOMA ACTION, with support from UN OCHA is involved in emergency water provision in drought-afflicted settlements of ElWaqdistrict.

Together with our International partners, SAIF has successfully implemented dozens of lifesaving projects. Projects implemented over the years include the construction of water storage and sanitation facilities, pit latrines, cash relief, distribution of NFI's, and the management/renovation of schools and health sanitation facilities. We attribute our program's success to lasting relations with communities, beneficiaries, and other stakeholders with whom we share our goal of delivering timely and relevant programming.

Soma-Action has an establishment of good working relationship with communities- community acceptance and Recommendations from local Administration. SAIF has been trained in UNICEF’s WASH technical designs and the CLTS/ODF approach. Besides the construction of WASH facilities, the SAIF has acquired skills in the implementation of ODF strategy and one of its staff is a UNICEF trainer, in rolling out ODF training of facilitators across Somalia. SAIF in 2014 concluded the first phase of the CLTS/ODF flagship project in Gedo region. SAIF has been partnering with UNICEF for the implementation of a resilience program in Elwaq and has also been the District focal point for AWD and flood response.

The cross-border peace-building initiatives have also helped the waring community able to live together and share mega resources like water and pasture despite of constant drought/floods experienced in the region. SAIF has a lot of experience in implementing programs in Somalia and this is widely contributed by a highly experienced team of experts in this field of emergency and development programming.
