WHAT WE DO

Cash Transfer

Cash transfers are direct payments of money to eligible individuals or households.

Conditional Cash Transfers (CCTs)

  • Conditional Cash Transfers (CCTs) are part of social protection strategies in many countries.
  • CCTs target poor households and are conditional on specific investments in education or health. For example, households may receive cash if they keep children in school or attend prenatal check-ups.
  • The goal is to reduce current poverty and promote future poverty reduction by encouraging investments in human capital.
  • CCTs have documented impacts on reducing poverty, increasing school participation, reducing child labor, and improving health and nutrition services for mothers and children. However, evidence for longer-term transformative change among program beneficiaries is limited.

Unconditional Cash Transfers (UCTs)

  • Unconditional Cash Transfers (UCTs) are becoming more popular, especially in low-income settings where conditionalities are harder to implement.
  • Recent studies have debunked stereotypes about cash transfers causing dependency, increasing consumption of temptation goods, or reducing labor-market participation.
  • In humanitarian crises, cash transfers can provide assistance by securely providing money to those who need it most when goods are available but people have lost the means to buy them.
